## Generative Simulation

The brain never perceives reality directly. It operates with a **generative model** — an internal simulation continuously updated based on sensory input.

- What you "see" is a prediction, not raw data from your eyes
- Sensory input serves only as a **correction signal** (prediction error)
- Two people in the same room "see" different situations — different generative models, different priors, different emotional weights

**Foundation:** Karl Friston (Free Energy Principle), Anil Seth ("controlled hallucination"), Andy Clark (predictive processing).

**Tier 1** — strong evidence.

## Subjective Experience: Two-Source Hypothesis

Subjective experience is not an extra layer on top of data processing — it **is** the way biological systems process data. But it has two components:

### Source 1: Interoceptively Dependent

- Somatic markers, gut feelings, body-anchored emotions
- The body continuously generates a stream of interoceptive signals (heart rate, muscle tone, breathing, hormonal levels, gut motility)
- The brain compresses this into a feeling — "I'm fine", "something's wrong", "I want to leave"
- **Evidence:** Patients with complete spinal cord transection report blunted emotional experience proportional to the level of injury (Hohmann, 1966)
- **Collapses in bvFTD** — interoceptive and emotional systems fail together (Ibáñez et al., 2024 meta-analysis, 170 studies, 7,032 participants)

### Source 2: Interoceptively Independent

- Constructed emotions, cognitive appraisal, learned emotional patterns
- Survives even with interoceptive deficits
- **Evidence:** In Alzheimer's, interoceptive deficits occur WITHOUT emotional convergence — emotion operates on a separate substrate
- Supports Barrett's theory of constructed emotions and Damasio's somatic marker hypothesis as complementary, not competing

### Implication

The ratio between Source 1 and Source 2 shifts based on interoceptive data availability:
- Healthy person: Source 1 dominant (body-anchored)
- Progressive neurodegeneration: Source 2 compensates
- Advanced meditation: deliberate shift between sources

**Tier 2** — consistent with data, partially falsified (works for bvFTD, complicated by Alzheimer's), requires two-component refinement.

## Evolution Didn't Design Consciousness

Organisms whose neural architecture integrated sensory data into a coherent model had higher fitness. Evolution didn't plan consciousness — it selected architectures where integration emerged.

"Goal" is not a property of the organism — it's a retrospective description of selection pressure. Bacteria don't "want" to survive — their architecture is the result of billions of years of selection.

**Tier 1** (evolutionary principle) / **Tier 2** (application to consciousness).
