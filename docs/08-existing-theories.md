## Predictive Processing (Friston, Seth, Clark)

**Relationship:** Primary foundation of the model.

- Free Energy Principle and Active Inference provide the mathematical backbone
- "Controlled hallucination" (Seth) = our "generative simulation"
- Prediction error minimization = our predictive loop
- The model extends Friston by adding: interoceptive compression as the mechanism of subjective experience, inter-organismal synchronization as L5, and gradient descent as a general interaction principle

**Tier 1.**

## Integrated Information Theory — IIT (Tononi)

**Relationship:** Partial agreement, significant extension.

### Where We Agree

- Integration of information is necessary for consciousness
- Phi (integrated information) could operationalize what we call "sufficient complexity"
- IIT correctly predicts that cerebellum (more neurons than cortex but feed-forward architecture) has low consciousness — low Phi despite high neuron count

### Where We Diverge

- **IIT ignores the body.** Phi measures only neural architecture. Our model says consciousness = Phi × interoceptive compression × mode. A brain in a vat with high Phi but no body would have different (not necessarily lesser) consciousness.
- **IIT is anti-functionalist** — claims structure determines consciousness, not function. Two functionally identical systems with different architecture would have different Phi → different consciousness. Our model is functionalist.
- **IIT has no inter-organismal component.** Each system has its own Phi. Our model adds L5 — shared consciousness through synchronization.

## Penrose-Hameroff (Orchestrated Objective Reduction)

**Relationship:** Rejected.

- Claims consciousness arises from quantum computations in neuronal microtubules
- Penrose argument: Gödel's theorems → human thought is non-algorithmic → must be quantum
- The logical leap is enormous, and no one has demonstrated that microtubules maintain quantum coherence long enough to be functionally significant at 37°C
- Tegmark (2000) calculated quantum coherence in brain would decay in ~10⁻¹³ seconds. Neural processes operate on millisecond timescale — 10 orders of magnitude slower.
- Quantum effects in biology exist (tunneling in enzymes, coherence in photosynthesis) but these are quantum chemistry, not quantum consciousness.
- Fisher's Posner molecule hypothesis (nuclear spin of phosphorus maintaining coherence for hours) is interesting but speculative.

**Tier 1** (rejection is well-supported).

## Global Workspace Theory (Baars, Dehaene)

**Relationship:** Compatible.

- Information becomes "conscious" when it wins competition for access to the global workspace and is broadcast across cortex
- Maps well to our model: consciousness = when information passes the prediction error threshold and gets integrated into the generative model
- Our model adds: the competition is modulated by emotional tags (not just salience) and the broadcast medium includes interoceptive channels (not just cortical)

---

## Constructed Emotion Theory (Barrett)

**Relationship:** Integrated as core component.

- Emotions are not reactions to the world but **constructs** the brain generates for efficient allocation of bodily resources (energy budget)
- Directly supports our "emotions as fundamental encoding system"
- Barrett's framework + Damasio's somatic markers = our two-source hypothesis (interoceptively dependent + independent)

---

## Embodied Cognition (Varela, Thompson, Damasio)

**Relationship:** Strong alignment.

- Cognition is not just in the brain — it's in the body and the environment
- Damasio: without body, no self
- Our model extends this: body is the primary substrate, but not the only possible one (Hawking case — symbolic reallocation when body input is lost)
