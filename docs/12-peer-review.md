## Consensus — What Survived All Five Perspectives

- **Predictive loop** — most robust part of the model
- **Rejection of quantum consciousness** — correct identification of mainstream position
- **ADHD as different calibration** — supported with citations by both neuroscientist and clinician
- **Hard problem is not solved** — all reviewers agree the model dissolves rather than resolves it

---

## Consistent Criticism

1. **"Experience = emotional compression"** — philosopher: explanatory gap; neuroscientist: not formulated this way in literature; clinician: over-intellectualization risk
2. **Stochastic resonance → creativity** — physicist and neuroscientist: unsupported leap from signal detection to network bridging
3. **"Goal as organizing principle"** — evolutionary biologist: anthropomorphizes evolution; philosopher: panpsychism by the back door
4. **LSD boot sequence** — physicist: pharmacological artifact, not evolutionary recapitulation; clinician: over-interpretation of subjective experience

---

## Strongest Parts

- Predictive processing framework — solid scientific foundation
- ADHD/depression as different modes of the same system — clinically useful and research-supported
- Spectrum of consciousness states — consistent across perspectives
- Esotericism as pre-scientific neuroscience — interesting interpretive frame (with clinical risk caveat)

## Emergent Meta-Insight

No individual reviewer saw this, but it emerged from cross-perspective analysis:

> **The model is itself a demonstration of what it describes** — it's a generative simulation of reality created by a brain seeking a coherent predictive framework for its own functioning. An ADHD brain with strong pattern recognition created a model that is consistent with mainstream science but systematically overvalues its own insights (confirmation bias in the predictive model — exactly what the model describes).

## Verdict

Strong intuitive model that surprisingly well corresponds to current predictive processing science. Main value is as a **personal explanatory framework**. As a scientific theory, it would need formalization, falsifiable predictions, and systematic engagement with existing literature. Greatest risk: confusing "consistent with data" for "proven by data."
