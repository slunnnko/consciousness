# Hierarchical Distributed Consciousness

[← Back to README](../README.md)

---

## Core Claim

Consciousness is not localized in a single organ. It exists at multiple levels of a hierarchy simultaneously.

---

## Levels

| Level | System | Type | Latency | Autonomy |
|-------|--------|------|---------|----------|
| L0 | Cell / basic reflex | none | — | — |
| L1 | Spinal cord / peripheral NS | proto-consciousness | 25–50ms | full — operates without brain |
| L2 | Subcortical structures | pre-consciousness (emotional, automatic) | ~100ms | high — amygdala fires before cortex knows |
| L3 | Cortex + body | full consciousness | 300–500ms | moderate — can override L1/L2 at energy cost |
| L4 | Organism + environment | meta-consciousness (self-model) | — | depends on L3 |
| L5 | Inter-organismal | shared / synchronized consciousness | — | see [Synchronization](05-synchronization.md) |

---

## Key Properties

- **Each level operates autonomously.** Spinal reflex doesn't need cortex. Amygdala evaluates threat before conscious awareness.
- **Higher levels integrate outputs of lower levels**, but lower levels function without higher.
- **Decision speed is inversely proportional to level.** Reflex = 25ms. Conscious decision = 300–500ms.
- **Higher can override lower**, but at energy cost. (Consciously holding your hand in ice water overrides L1 withdrawal reflex.)

---

## Evidence from Reflex Arcs

- **Spinal reflex** (knee jerk): sensor → spinal cord → motor. ~30–50ms. Brain learns about it *after*. Round-trip to brain would take ~100–200ms.
- **Withdrawal reflex** (hand from stove): polysynaptic, spinal. ~80–100ms. You feel pain *after* you've already pulled away.
- **Monosynaptic stretch reflex**: fastest, ~25ms. No brain, no cerebellum, just spinal cord.
- The spinal cord has ~100 million neurons — more than the entire brain of many animals that exhibit complex behavior.

---

## The Octopus Test

The octopus is the key test case for this model:

- 2/3 of neurons are in the arms, not in the central brain
- An arm can independently search for food, open jars, respond to threats — **even when severed** from the body
- Each arm has its own decision-making capacity

### Model Prediction

- Octopus arm = L1–L2 consciousness (sensors + local integration + goal)
- Central brain = higher-level integration across arms
- Whole octopus = hierarchical system with **distributed consciousness at multiple levels**

This is a stronger claim than most consciousness theories make: consciousness isn't just in the brain, it's a property of any sufficiently integrated subsystem with sensory input and a selection-shaped "goal."

## Mode Interaction

L3 and L4 can switch between [modes](02-three-modes.md):
- Healthy person: body-anchored default
- Progressive input loss: symbolic reallocation
- Meditation: temporary shift to non-dual
- Sleep: reconfigures all levels (but L1 stays active — you turn in your sleep)
- Psychedelics: primarily disrupt L2–L3 configuration (prior precision drops)

**Tier 2** — hierarchy as concept. Tier 1 for individual components (reflex autonomy, subcortical speed).
