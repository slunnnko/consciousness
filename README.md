## Core Claims

1. **Consciousness is an emergent property** of a sufficiently complex nervous system. No special ingredient required.
2. **The brain generates a simulation of reality**, not direct perception. Sensory input is a correction signal, not the source.
3. **Subjective experience has two sources** — one body-dependent (somatic markers), one body-independent (constructed emotions).
4. **Evolution didn't design consciousness** — it selected architectures where data integration emerged.
5. **Consciousness exists in three modes** — body-anchored (default), symbolically reallocated (Hawking), and non-dual (advanced meditation).
6. **Brains synchronize during interaction** — measurable, robust, and modulated by relationship quality and channel count.
