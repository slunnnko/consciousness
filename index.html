A Functional Model of Consciousness

Jiří Polášek + Claude (Anthropic) · v2.3
Consciousness is an emergent property of complex nervous systems. The brain generates a simulation of reality — not direct perception. Subjective experience arises from two sources: body-anchored emotional compression and constructed cognition. Brains synchronize during interaction. The model is built from first principles and cross-referenced with neuroscience literature.
01

Core Model

  • Consciousness = emergent property of a sufficiently complex nervous system. No special ingredient required.
  • The brain generates a simulation, not direct perception. Sensory input = correction signal (prediction error), not source.
  • Subjective experience has two sources:

Source 1: Interoceptively Dependent

Somatic markers, gut feelings, body-anchored emotions. The body generates a continuous stream of signals — heart rate, muscle tone, breathing, hormones — and the brain compresses this into a feeling. Collapses when interoceptive and emotional systems fail together (bvFTD).

Source 2: Interoceptively Independent

Constructed emotions, cognitive appraisal, learned emotional patterns. Survives even without interoceptive data. In Alzheimer’s, interoceptive deficits occur without emotional convergence — emotion runs on a separate substrate.

Evidence: Ibáñez et al. (2024) meta-analysis — 170 studies, 7,032 participants across neurodegenerative diseases.

  • Evolution didn’t design consciousness — it selected architectures where data integration emerged.

  • Emotions are fast heuristics — amygdala ~12ms, conscious processing ~300ms
  • Memory = reconstruction from emotional tag, not file retrieval. False memories exist because the brain reconstructs from emotion, not data.
  • ADHD pattern: routine = low precision (boring). Deviations = high precision (interesting). Strong pattern recognition, weak delayed recall.

Roles as Filters

Playing a role filters your authentic signal → the other person synchronizes with the role, not you. Intimacy requires vulnerability = lowering filters. Long relationships accumulate defenses → block sync → models diverge.

Attachment

Newborn builds its generative model from maternal mirroring. If mother doesn’t mirror (depression, absence, trauma) → model calibrates to “my signals are irrelevant” → lifelong pattern.

Calibration

Genetics → baseline weights. Epigenetics + environment → fine-tuning. Two people in the same situation see different realities.

ADHD as Precision Weighting

Novelty: high precision. Routine: low precision. Emotional threshold: higher than neurotypical — only intense signals pass. Dopamine modulates precision — low dopamine = high threshold = need for extremes.

Medication prediction: Concerta/Medikinet lowers threshold → subtler signals pass → less need for extremes. Not personality change — filter recalibration.

Inter-Generational Transfer

Cultural norms + epigenetics shape which signals are systematically suppressed across generations. Stress responses transfer transgenerationally (Yehuda et al. — Holocaust survivor descendants). Not deterministic — sets predisposition.

Open Questions

  2. Threshold: Where in L1–L5 does subjective experience begin?
  3. Mode relationship: Are non-dual (meditation) and symbolic (Hawking) modes related or different?
  4. Roles as filters: Can we measure this via hyperscanning (authenticity vs. role-play)?
  5. Animal consciousness: Does the model scale? Octopus, birds, insects?
  6. Two-source falsification: Is Source 2 truly body-independent, or dependent at a deeper level (hormonal)?
